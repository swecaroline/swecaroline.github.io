# NEW BLOOD JOINS THIS EARTH (2024)

A heavy metal data visualization and interactive essay.
<hr>

**Role:** Personal project
<hr>

**Tech stack:** AnyChart.js, Flourish, React.js, Spotify API, Python
<hr>

**Approaches:** Data visualization, digital storytelling, web scraping
<hr>

I developed a data visualization about the international, inter-generational, cross-genre influence of Metallica, analyzing the 55 songs on Metallica's cover compailation *The Metallica Blacklist* (2021) celebrating thirty years of their eponymous "Black Album." 

<a class="button m-auto center" href="https://swecaroline.github.io/metallica-blacklist" target="_blank">Explore project</a>
<a class="button m-auto center" href="https://github.com/swecaroline/metallica-blacklist" target="_blank">Browse code</a>

<hr>

## My process

I gathered the data from three sources: the Spotify API, an archive of [everynoise.com](https://everynoise.com){:target="_blank"}, and publicly-available information from the Internet. For the first two, I wrote 
Python scripts - I created a script to query the Spotify API, and another script to scrape the HTML from a 
Wayback Machine link - to compile and format my data for visualization. This data went into the first and last charts in the visualization. 

I then created Excel sheets to manually gather artists' biographical information and quotes from critic reviews. This data 
formed the second and third charts in the visualization.  

I based the visual aesthetics of the site on the packaging design of Metallica's albums, and on the band's [archival collections](https://web.archive.org/web/20240424170825/http://metallicablackbox.com/){:target="_blank"}.
